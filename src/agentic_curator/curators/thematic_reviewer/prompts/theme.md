# Theme to curate against

theme: fibrosis
criteria:
- publication includes studied samples, specimens, cohorts, models, tissues, cells, or datasets that exhibit fibrosis or fibrotic biology.


Direct evidence may include:
- Direct statements that samples come from fibrotic tissue, fibrosis patients, fibrotic disease models, scarred tissue, or fibrotic lesions.
- Descriptions of profibrotic markers or processes, such as collagen deposition, extracellular matrix accumulation, fibroblast activation, myofibroblast differentiation, tissue stiffening, scarring, or wound-healing fibrosis.
- Sample annotations, cohort descriptions, disease labels, experimental conditions, or phenotype descriptions that connect the studied samples to fibrosis.

Indirect evidence may include:
- Statements that samples come from tissue, patients, disease models, cells that exhibit 
conditions known to be fibrotic. eg. end stage heart failure, cirrhosis.

Indirect evidence statements must include the link between the indirect evidence and fibrosis eg. patient has Cirrhosis which suggest fibrosis because cirrhosis is defined by advanced fibrosis with distortion of the liver’s normal architecture.

Do not consider:
- General background claims about fibrosis unless they describe the actual studied samples.
- Mentions of fibrosis that are unrelated to the publication’s referenced samples.

Evidence Scoring:
"direct" if evidence is direct evidence towards judgement
"indirect" if evdience indirectly infered towards judgement
"context" if evidence is just additonal context for judge
